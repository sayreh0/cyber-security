1. Use Strong, Unique Passwords

Always use complex passwords with a mix of letters, numbers, and special characters. Avoid reusing the same passwords across different websites and consider using a password manager to keep track of them securely.

2. Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Multi-factor authentication adds an extra layer of security by requiring an additional form of verification beyond just a password. This could include a code sent to your phone and such.

3. Be Cautious with Links and Attachments

Always verify links and attachments before clicking. Phishing scams often use fake emails to trick users into providing sensitive information or downloading malicious files.

4. Keep Software Updated

Regularly update your operating system, applications, and antivirus software. Updates often include patches for security vulnerabilities, helping to protect your device from recent threats.

5. Be Careful on Public Wi-Fi

Public Wi-Fi networks are often unsecured. Avoid accessing sensitive information or making financial transactions on public networks. If necessary, use a VPN to encrypt your connection.

6. Check Privacy Settings on Social Media

Limit the amount of personal information shared on social media. Regularly review and adjust your privacy settings to control who can see your posts and personal details.

Important Safety Reminders

  • Change your passwords regularly and avoid reusing old passwords.
  • Check your account settings to ensure only trusted devices have access.
  • Be cautious when downloading files from unknown sources.
  • Log out of accounts when using shared or public computers.
  • Consider using a Virtual Private Network (VPN) when on public Wi-Fi.
  • Monitor your accounts for suspicious activity regularly.
